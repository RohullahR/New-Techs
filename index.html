Onboarding Roadmap

Step 1: Orientation & Setup

We’ll start your first day by introducing you to the team, showing you around the workspace—including areas like Inventory and Quality Control—and getting your systems and access set up. We'll also make sure you receive all the tools and equipment you need to begin your work confidently.

Step 2: Process Training

You'll get to know our core RepairGenie (RG) system. You'll learn how to log warranty vs. non-warranty devices and understand damage codes like ADW, CLW, CID...

Step 3: Hands-on Training Begins

Time to get practical! You'll begin with basic repairs—such as replacing LCD screens, keyboards, and LCD base covers—under the supervision of experienced staff. As you settle in, you’ll build your technical skills through guided, hands-on practice and close collaboration with senior technicians, observing their work and gradually taking on more tasks as your confidence grows.

Step 4: Supervised Practice

At this stage, you'll begin working on devices with simple issues. The key focus will be following the documented workflow, keeping precise records, and reporting accurately in the RG system.

Next Step: Independence & Growth

Once you've completed your assigned course and received approval from the technical lead, you'll begin working independently on more complex tasks. If you're unsure which courses to take, click here to see the list. Click here to see the list.

Daily Checklist

  • Pre-QC all devices before sending them to the QC station.
  • Return remaining devices to their designated shelf.
  • Label returnable parts: Include damage type, device model, and your credential number on the bag.
  • Ensure all parts are scanned right after collecting them from inventory.
  • Tidy up your workstation before clocking out.
